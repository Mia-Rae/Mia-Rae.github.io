How to be a decent DnD player

Basic Ideas and Equitette

Making decisions: What would my character do?
  • Goals / Wishes
  • Needs & Values
  • Weaknesses
On being less selfish / spotlight-y
  • Try interacting and asking (quiet) party members (eg. "hey, you were so good at the bar yesterday, you mind haggling with npc, I'm stuck")
  • try involving others (wingman, consoling, ...) when having an emotional scene (flirting, sad, ...); try and pass the ball to others in conversations
  • if someones getting interrupted, try and bring the conversation back to them (after the interruption topic is resolved)
  • be an observer of scenes I'm not involved in
Supporting the DM (time to chill, listen, recover or prepare)
  • keep track of your own shit and takes notes (de-buffs, stats, ...)
  • create a rough session log (eg. so you can do a "replay" in next session), esp. the random answers like npc names, etc.

  • When ending a session, get chars to talk about rough (quest-)plans for the future, so DM has inspiration and ideas for the planning

  • get the group to take the lead in (some) RP moments, so

  • Ask the group instead of the DM first
Initiating pc roleplay / relationship building

  • prepare notes for every char for what we have in common, to use this whenever possible to try and bond over it

  • After big battle, reflect and check in emotionally with other chars

  • Ask chars for help and advice (about game but also emotional from my chars backgroung)
  • Suggest bets or dares between chars (if their backstory is competitive)
  • Offer help (but twisted -> goal is to create story arches my char is not really involved with): eg. feel pc -> npc sparks, but they are oblvious so far? Offer being a wingman, then see it unfold

Roleplay better

  • say what your character says / thinks. Play out any interaction as a conversation.
Giving your character a voice

Finding a voice

  • physicality: pitch & formants
  • personality: sexy, exited, shy, sharp, stutter ... -> brainstorm how the trains of char might sound
    • super quick, super slow, everything as question, everything as a statement, ...
  • quirks: like apologising a lot or saying 'like' a bunch, making puns or innuendos

How to use the voice

  • practice (outside of sessions)
  • find a phrase to get into character ("fire from heat, heat from fire")
  • be brave, people will love it
Understanding your characters motivation & personality
  • what are my characters goals right now
  • what are the traits, weaknesses
  • how does my char perform my action
  • how does my char sound
